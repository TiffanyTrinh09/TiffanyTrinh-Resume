I am currently majoring in biochemistry in hopes of becoming a pediatrician in the future. In my junior year of high school, I was so excited about following the pre-med track that I decided to take an anatomy and physiology course. Through this class, I was able to learn medical terminology about the human body systems as well as its functions. To enhance my understanding, I got to dissect a variety of animals to learn about the different anatomy structures such as fish, frogs, sharks, lamb heart, and cow eye. I also gained insight into many different medical career paths which also included the type of work they do.

During high school, I got to partake in a new biomedical program that allowed students like me to conduct scientific experiments to solve real life situations. For example, my teacher recreated the crime scene for us students to critically think and analyze what had happened to the victim. We were able to examine the crime scene such as the blood, fingerprint, footprint, weapon, and medication. In order to understand the events that lead to the crime, we had to learn how to perform the PCR process as well as DNA fingerprinting. Throughout this course, I was able to learn forensic science, analyze data, and develop technical skills.

Towards the beginning of my senior year, I also joined the Medical Assistant Clinic course to further my knowledge and skills in the healthcare field. In class, my teacher taught us how to assess a patient's vital signs. This procedure included both the traditional and modern way. I learned how to measure their pulse, blood pressure, respiration rate, and temperature. I also learned how to use the traditional scale and electronic scale to measure the height and weight of the patient. In addition, this course gave me an opportunity to learn how to take glucose level tests as well as how to withdraw blood.
